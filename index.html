Nuclear Power is Often Misunderstood

Nuclear power is the key to slowing climate change and moving away from fossil fules.
However its often misunderstood and thought to be very dangerous.
But countries across the globe have already started to adopt nuclear power as the future of energy.

When we burn fossil fuels for electricity they give off greenhouse gasses. These greenhouse gasses move up into the atmosphere and trap the heat from the sun, this creates a greenhouse effect where more heat is coming in than is getting out which causes global warming.
By burning fossil fuels we released 36.8 billion metric tons of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere in 2023 alone. This is a huge part of why every year it feels like we are setting new records for the hottest summer. If this continues then it's only going to get hotter and hotter untill it becomes imposible to grow crops or provide enough food for everyone.

1 kilogram of natural uranium is able to produce the same amount of electricity as 20 metric tons of coal without producing any greenhouse gasses. Nuclear power also doesn't require as much space as solar or wind turbines do. It is one of the cleanest and most effective energy sources we have right now and in the US in 2023 We produced 18% of our power with nuclear energy, so we are already on our way! However we still need to work hard to get that number up. It's important for future generations that we start acting now to slow down or even stop climate change before it's too late.

  • Public Perception

    • Due to misinformation and accidents like chernobyl and fukushima lots of people are scared of nuclear power plants but with how strict the safety policies are now these kinds of accidents are unlikely.

  • High Initial Costs

    • Nuclear plants are expensive to build upfront, however they don't take as much land as other renewables and they create lots of job openings from construction jobs to helping run the plant after its been built.
